How does this work?

Step 1: I converted AQI back to PM 2.5 particle concentration. That's the number of particles in the air that are a certain size. For this operation I used the same equation as the Air Now calculator.

Step 2: based on this research, the health impact of a particle concentration of 22μg/m3 per 24 hours is equivalent to about 1 cigarette. I divided the concentration from step 1 by 22 and 24, then multiplied by the number of hours exposed.

Step 3: the final result is rounded to the nearest integer. Because we are looking at impact to health, not particles inhaled, we aren't measuring something precise and objective. In converting AQI to PM2.5 we lose a lot of information. Different types of air pollution can also be better or worse for you than cigarettes. A previous version of this calculator showed two decimal places, but I decided this could be misleading and opted to show an integer instead.
